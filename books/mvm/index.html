About the Book

Understanding and accommodating neurodivergent employees is essential for modern workplace success. Modernising HR for Variant Minds is the streamlined, easy-reading companion to Employing Variant Minds, designed specifically for HR managers and corporate leadership. This concise pocket guide distils the most crucial insights from its comprehensive counterpart, providing actionable strategies for fostering an inclusive and high-performing workplace.

Covering key neurodivergent conditions such as Autism, ADHD, and AuDHD, this book highlights the fundamental workplace challenges neurodivergent employees face and presents practical solutions tailored for business leaders. With a focus on modernising HR policies, Modernising HR for Variant Minds challenges outdated recruitment practices, performance assessments, and workplace biases that may unintentionally exclude talented professionals.

HR professionals and leaders will gain the knowledge to recognise and support neurodivergent employees, implement effective workplace adjustments, and eliminate unconscious biases that hinder innovation. With concise, real-world examples and straightforward tools, this book equips decision-makers with the essential knowledge needed to create a truly inclusive and productive workplace.

Modernising HR for Variant Minds is the go-to resource for executives and HR professionals seeking a quick yet comprehensive guide to harnessing the full potential of neurodivergent talent in the workforce.

About the Author

Silvino Rodrigues

With over 30 years of experience in corporate technology firms, Silvino Rodrigues brings with him knowledge from various domains, including software compiler technology, distributed computing applications, embedded systems electronics, large data centre facilities, and skyscraper refurbishment project management. Fueled by a relentless quest for new knowledge, he possesses a diverse educational background that includes tertiary studies in electronics engineering, financial management, and an MBA, along with more recent studies in adult education and psychology.

His late dual diagnosis of ASD (L-2:2) and ADHD (PI) explains his constant pursuit of new challenges, often at the expense of fully enjoying the rewards of his efforts. Now he has committed his life to assisting neurodivergent adults in navigating the realm of corporate citizenship — showing how neurodivergent individuals can thrive in a world that has yet to understand their minds.
